# Diagnostic Design Partner Outreach Toolkit
**Use:** W1 outreach to recruit 3–5 pilot design partners.  
**Guideline:** Invite to “co-shape” the diagnostic system, not “try a product.” Position as low-effort diagnostic in exchange for feedback.  
**Tone:** Consultative, experienced, outcome-oriented. Avoid hype words.

---

## Touchpoint 1: LinkedIn / Email (Inquiry)

**Subject:** Would you co-design the next version of operational diagnosis?  
**Message:**  
I’m RING of Phoenix AI Consulting. We’ve spent years building diagnosis-to-strategy frameworks with enterprise ops teams, and we’re now packaging that into a structured diagnostic workflow — not a chat bot, but a rigorous problem-to-decision system with consultant accountability built in.

I’m looking for 3–5 design partners who will receive an early pilot at a reduced engagement fee in exchange for structured feedback on what’s useful and what isn’t.

If you’ve dealt with recurring operational problems, AI adoption decisions, or cross-functional decision delays in the past year, I’d value 20 minutes to see if there’s a fit.

— RING

---

## Touchpoint 2: Follow-up (Text / Short)

**Message:**  
Quick follow-up: we’re not asking for commitment to buy. We’re testing if a structured diagnostic + consultant-signed output is actually useful for your team. Early pilots also get priority scheduling for Q3 engagements.

---

## Touchpoint 3: Meeting Agenda (30 min)

1. Introduce Phoenix diagnostic framework  
2. Walk through sample diagnostic output (anonymized)  
3. Understand their current problem-solving workflow  
4. Define one candidate problem for pilot scope  
5. Discuss design-partner terms: reduced fee + feedback cadence  
6. Decide: proceed to scoped intake or pass

---

## Evaluation Criteria for Design Partners

- [ ] Has a current, real problem that is bounded and decision-relevant  
- [ ] Willing to share expert context and confirm factual accuracy of outputs  
- [ ] Has budget authority or influence for NT$150K–500K diagnostic engagements  
- [ ] Will commit to feedback loop (2 structured check-ins + post-delivery debrief)

---

## Call Script (Voice)

**Opening:**  
“Hi, this is RING from Phoenix AI Consulting. We’re running a small program with ops and executive teams to test a new diagnostic workflow that turns messy operational problems into structured, consultant-signed decision documents. Is this the kind of thing that would be useful for your organization?”

**If interested:**  
“Great — I’d propose a 20-minute screen share next week to show you what the output looks like. We’ll also discuss whether we can structure it as a low-cost design-partner pilot for Q3.”

**Objection — “What’s the cost?”**  
“It’s a reduced pilot fee — between NT$150,000 and the standard diagnostic range. You get an actual usable deliverable; we get structured feedback. No obligation beyond that.”

**Objection — “Is this AI chatbot?”**  
“No. It’s a framework-rigorous diagnostic workflow where the system does retrieval and structuring, but the final answer is signed by our consultant. You get a decision-ready document with accountability.”

---

## Next Actions After Outreach

1. Book 30-min scope call within 5 business days.  
2. Send intake template and 2-page sample output after the call.  
3. Issue pilot SOW for internal review same week.  
4. Begin asset write-back process upon delivery.
